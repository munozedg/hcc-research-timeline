Texas leads the nation in hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) incidence, driven by the highest concentration of Hispanic Americans, high rates of metabolic disease, and legacy hepatitis B/C burden. Since 2009, CPRIT has invested $59–77 million in liver cancer research, building a world-class network across MD Anderson, BCM, UTSW, UTHealth, and UT Health San Antonio. Dr. Amelie G. Ramirez (UTHSCSA / IHPR) led the foundational epidemiological studies establishing that South Texas Latinos bear the nation’s highest HCC burden — 3–4× higher rates than non-Hispanic Whites — and discovered the first U.S. link between aflatoxin exposure and liver cancer in Latinos.
